Have we as a society become so overwhelmed by the sheer volume of information that we gravitate towards the simplest of means to understand it all? In that process, do we create a never-ending cycle of technological dependency and oversimplification?  Does reducing a complex concept or story down to a meme or emoji contribute to a larger societal problem as Nicholas Carr calls “pancake people” in his book The Shallows? One could argue that treating sensationalistic headlines as whole facts and a love of meme culture has contributed to the rise of fake news, oversimplified truths, and easily debunked conspiracy theories that have plagued society in recent years.
