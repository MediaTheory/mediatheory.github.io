The average adult American spends around 33 hours a week doing mundane tasks online, whether it be general web surfing, emails, and/or social media. (Choudhury 5). The more we as a culture are exposed to shorter oversimplified forms of communication, the more we seek and have less tolerance for longer delayed gratifications that complex forms of information require. The ping of a text message creates a Pavlovian response where we seek more of the same to gratify ourselves. Longer more nuanced versions of news stories become too cumbersome and less satisfactory. Choudhury and McKinney explain that the “dopaminergic receptors in [our] prefrontal and paralimbic areas” are related to sensation seeking (5).
