As our neural pathways seek out these sources of dopamine, the more we look for shorter simplified narratives and more audiovisual sensations. The key chemical of dopamine helps regulate our prefrontal cortex- the region responsible for executive function and responsible decision-making. It becomes a complex cycle of simplifying narratives into short sound bites. While there’s a focus on adolescent brains that have incomplete prefrontal cortexes, the same phenomenon happens with adult brains. There are also theories that this surge of dopamine can interfere with executive functions to varying degrees- primarily with filtering irrelevant information out. While these theories obviously require more research, it helps explain the perfect storm for easily debunked “fake news” to be believed even among highly educated populations of adults.
